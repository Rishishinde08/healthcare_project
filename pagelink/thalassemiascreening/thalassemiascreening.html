Why Thalassaemia Screening Should Be a Priority Before Marriage and Pregnancy

By Dr. Satyaranjan Das in Hematology Oncology
May 19, 2025   |   5 min read

Planning a family is a special and life-changing experience, but it also comes with important responsibilities. One of the most crucial steps in this process is to ensure that both partners are aware of potential health risks that could affect their future children. Among these risks, thalassaemia is a serious condition that can impact the health of a child and the well-being of the entire family. This is why thalassaemia screening should be a priority before marriage and pregnancy. Thalassaemia is a genetic blood disorder that affects the production of hemoglobin. Both partners may unknowingly carry the thalassaemia gene, and without screening, they might be unaware of the risk they face in having a child with this condition.

Thalassaemia screening before marriage and pregnancy is crucial for couples who are planning to start a family. By identifying the risks early, couples can reduce the emotional, financial, and physical strain that often accompanies raising a child with this genetic condition. Raising awareness about thalassaemia screening and empowering couples with knowledge allows them to make informed decisions that will lead to healthier, more prepared families.
